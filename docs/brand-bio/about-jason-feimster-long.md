## Positioning Summary
Jason Feimster is a U.S. Marine combat veteran turned entrepreneur, operator, affiliate-growth strategist, and AI systems builder. He works at the intersection of AI leverage, alternative finance, no-code automation, entrepreneurial media, and audience-owned digital assets.

His work is built for people who are capable, ambitious, and often under-supported: founders, freelancers, gig workers, eCommerce sellers, consultants, real estate operators, and independent business builders trying to create more ownership, more leverage, and less dependence on gatekeepers.

Rather than selling polished startup fantasy, Jason focuses on practical business infrastructure: prompts, workflows, systems, content assets, positioning, funding strategy, and execution frameworks that help self-starters move from chaos to momentum.

## Core Areas of Work

### AI for real operators
Jason’s AI work is grounded in practical use cases rather than hype. He develops and explores prompts, systems, and workflow logic that help founders and operators create more output without needing a giant team or a computer-science degree.

### Alternative finance and capital strategy
Through Moonshine Capital and related work, Jason operates close to the world of business funding, partner-led growth, and entrepreneur-facing capital education. His work helps founders think more clearly about non-traditional funding paths, leverage, and business infrastructure.

### Founder-led media and authority building
Jason creates and structures content designed to do more than just attract attention. He treats content as an asset: something that should sharpen positioning, build trust, support search visibility, and feed long-term ecosystem growth.

### No-code systems and operational infrastructure
A consistent theme in Jason’s work is turning loose ideas into practical systems. That includes site architecture, workflows, prompts, lead-gen structures, intake logic, brand documentation, and operational frameworks that make businesses easier to run.
