## Ecosystem Overview
Jason Feimster’s brand ecosystem is not just a set of disconnected projects. It is a layered system with distinct roles:

- **personal brand / thought leadership**
- **business and funding ecosystem**
- **venture / umbrella / incubation layer**
- **public channels and media distribution**

The goal is to make these pieces reinforce one another rather than compete for attention.

## How These Should Be Used in Public Copy

### On the About page
Lead with Jason as the operator and human being. Reference Full-Time Hustler, Moonshine Capital, and Juliet Foxtrot Ventures as parts of a broader ecosystem, not as a confusing list of logos.

### On the Services page
Use Moonshine Capital and Jason’s systems/operator framing more heavily than the venture-studio layer.

### On the Portfolio page
This file should become the backbone of the ecosystem map and portfolio explanation.

### On social/profile bios
Do not overstuff very short bios with all three brand names unless the context actually benefits from it.

### On docs/directory pages
Use this file to clarify how collections and brand layers relate to one another.

## Architecture Notes
- Full-Time Hustler should remain the strongest expression of the personal editorial voice.
- Moonshine Capital should remain the strongest expression of business-facing operator and funding credibility.
- Juliet Foxtrot Ventures should remain the strategic umbrella, not necessarily the first brand every visitor sees.
- The personal brand OS site should explain the system cleanly without making it feel fragmented or over-engineered.

## Guardrails
- Do not invent launch dates, audience numbers, or traction metrics unless verified.
- Do not overcomplicate the distinction between brands in user-facing copy.
- Keep descriptions specific enough to be useful but broad enough to remain valid as projects evolve.
- If a new brand or platform becomes important later, update this file before updating site/category pages.
